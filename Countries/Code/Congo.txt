<p>The <b>Republic of the Congo</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>République du Congo</i></span>), also known as <b>Congo Republic</b><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> or <b>Congo-Brazzaville</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">country</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central Africa</a>. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angolan</a> <a href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave" class="mw-redirect">exclave</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cabinda_Province" title="Cabinda Province">Cabinda</a>.</p>
<p>The region was dominated by <a href="/wiki/Bantu_peoples" title="Bantu peoples">Bantu</a>-speaking tribes, who built trade links leading into the <a href="/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a> basin. Congo-Brazzaville was formerly part of the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> colony of <a href="/wiki/French_Equatorial_Africa" title="French Equatorial Africa">Equatorial Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Upon independence in 1960, the former colony of <a href="/wiki/French_Congo" title="French Congo">French Congo</a> became the Republic of the Congo. The <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="People's Republic of the Congo">People's Republic of the Congo</a> was a Marxist–Leninist <a href="/wiki/Single-party_state" title="Single-party state">single-party state</a> from 1970 to 1991. Multi-party elections have been held since 1992, although a democratically elected government was ousted in the 1997 <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo_Civil_War" title="Republic of the Congo Civil War">Republic of the Congo Civil War</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="History of the Republic of the Congo">History of the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-colonial">Pre-colonial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-colonial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bantu_peoples" title="Bantu peoples">Bantu-speaking peoples</a> who founded tribes during the <a href="/wiki/Bantu_expansion" title="Bantu expansion">Bantu expansions</a> largely displaced and absorbed the earliest inhabitants of the region, the <a href="/wiki/Pygmy" title="Pygmy" class="mw-redirect">Pygmy</a> people, about 1500 BC. The <a href="/wiki/Bakongo" title="Bakongo" class="mw-redirect">Bakongo</a>, a Bantu ethnicity that also occupied parts of present-day Angola, Gabon and Democratic Republic of the Congo, formed the basis for ethnic affinities and rivalries among those countries. Several Bantu kingdoms—notably those of the <a href="/wiki/Kongo_Empire" title="Kongo Empire" class="mw-redirect">Kongo</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Loango" title="Kingdom of Loango">Loango</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Anziku" title="Kingdom of Anziku" class="mw-redirect">Teke</a>—built trade links leading into the Congo River basin.<sup id="cite_ref-dos2009_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dos2009-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Court_of_Loango.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Court_of_Loango.png/220px-Court_of_Loango.png" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Court_of_Loango.png/330px-Court_of_Loango.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Court_of_Loango.png/440px-Court_of_Loango.png 2x" data-file-width="1276" data-file-height="862" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Court_of_Loango.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The court of <a href="/wiki/N%27Gangue_M%27voumbe_Niambi" title="N'Gangue M'voumbe Niambi">N'Gangue M'voumbe Niambi</a>, from the book <i>Description of Africa</i> (1668)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Portugal" title="Kingdom of Portugal">Portuguese</a> explorer <a href="/wiki/Diogo_C%C3%A3o" title="Diogo Cão">Diogo Cão</a> reached the mouth of the Congo in 1484.<sup id="cite_ref-DicImp_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DicImp-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Commercial relationships quickly grew up between the inland Bantu kingdoms and European merchants who traded various commodities, manufactured goods, and slaves captured from the hinterlands. For centuries the Congo river delta served as a major commercial hub for transatlantic trade. However, direct European colonization of the area began in the late 19th century and eroded the power of the Bantu societies in the region.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="French_colonial_era">French colonial era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: French colonial era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The area north of the Congo River came under French sovereignty in 1880 as a result of <a href="/wiki/Pierre_de_Brazza" title="Pierre de Brazza" class="mw-redirect">Pierre de Brazza</a>'s treaty with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Makoko_(chief)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Makoko (chief) (page does not exist)">Makoko</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Bateke" title="Bateke" class="mw-redirect">Bateke</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DicImp_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DicImp-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> This Congo Colony became known first as <b><a href="/wiki/French_Congo" title="French Congo">French Congo</a></b>, then as <b><a href="/wiki/Middle_Congo" title="Middle Congo" class="mw-redirect">Middle Congo</a></b> in 1903. In 1908, France organized <a href="/wiki/French_Equatorial_Africa" title="French Equatorial Africa">French Equatorial Africa</a> (AEF), comprising Middle Congo, <a href="/wiki/French_Gabon" title="French Gabon" class="mw-redirect">Gabon</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Chad" title="French Chad">Chad</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oubangui-Chari" title="Oubangui-Chari" class="mw-redirect">Oubangui-Chari</a> (the modern <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>). The French designated <a href="/wiki/Brazzaville" title="Brazzaville">Brazzaville</a> as the federal capital. Economic development during the first 50 years of colonial rule in Congo centered on natural-resource extraction. The methods were often brutal: establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Congo%E2%80%93Ocean_Railroad" title="Congo–Ocean Railroad" class="mw-redirect">Congo–Ocean Railroad</a> following <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> has been estimated to have cost at least 14,000 lives.<sup id="cite_ref-DicImp_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DicImp-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_occupation_of_France" title="Nazi occupation of France" class="mw-redirect">Nazi occupation of France</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Brazzaville functioned as the symbolic capital of <a href="/wiki/Free_France" title="Free France">Free France</a> between 1940 and 1943.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Brazzaville_Conference_of_1944" title="Brazzaville Conference of 1944">Brazzaville Conference of 1944</a> heralded a period of major reform in French colonial policy. Congo benefited from the postwar expansion of colonial administrative and infrastructure spending as a result of its central geographic location within AEF and the federal capital at Brazzaville.<sup id="cite_ref-dos2009_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dos2009-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It also received a local legislature after the adoption of the 1946 constitution that established the <a href="/wiki/French_Fourth_Republic" title="French Fourth Republic">Fourth Republic</a>.</p>
<p>Following the revision of the French constitution that established the <a href="/wiki/French_Fifth_Republic" title="French Fifth Republic">Fifth Republic</a> in 1958, the AEF dissolved into its constituent parts, each of which became an autonomous colony within the <a href="/wiki/French_Community" title="French Community">French Community</a>. During these reforms, Middle Congo became known as the Republic of the Congo in 1958<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and published its first constitution in 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Antagonism between the pro-<a href="/wiki/Jacques_Opangault" title="Jacques Opangault">Opangault</a> <a href="/wiki/Mbochi" title="Mbochi" class="mw-redirect">Mbochis</a> and the pro-<a href="/wiki/Fulbert_Youlou" title="Fulbert Youlou">Youlou</a> Balalis resulted in a series of riots in Brazzaville in February 1959, which the <a href="/wiki/French_Army" title="French Army">French Army</a> subdued.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-independence_era">Post-independence era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-independence era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:214px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alphonse_Massamba-Debat.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2d/Alphonse_Massamba-Debat.png" width="212" height="286" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="212" data-file-height="286" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alphonse_Massamba-Debat.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Alphonse_Massamba-D%C3%A9bat" title="Alphonse Massamba-Débat">Alphonse Massamba-Débat</a>'s one-party rule (1963-1968) attempted to implement a <a href="/wiki/Political_economy" title="Political economy">political economic</a> strategy of "<a href="/wiki/Scientific_socialism" title="Scientific socialism">scientific socialism</a>."</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Republic of the Congo received full independence from France on August 15, 1960. <a href="/wiki/Fulbert_Youlou" title="Fulbert Youlou">Fulbert Youlou</a> ruled as the country's first president until labour elements and rival political parties instigated a <a href="/wiki/Trois_Glorieuses_(1963)" title="Trois Glorieuses (1963)">three-day uprising</a> that ousted him. The Congolese military took charge of the country briefly and installed a civilian provisional government headed by <a href="/wiki/Alphonse_Massamba-D%C3%A9bat" title="Alphonse Massamba-Débat">Alphonse Massamba-Débat</a>.</p>
<p>Under the 1963 constitution, Massamba-Débat was elected President for a five-year term.<sup id="cite_ref-dos2009_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dos2009-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> During <a href="/wiki/Alphonse_Massamba-D%C3%A9bat#Congo_under_Massamba-D.C3.A9bat_.281963-1968.29" title="Alphonse Massamba-Débat">Massamba-Débat's term in office</a> the regime adopted "<a href="/wiki/Scientific_socialism" title="Scientific socialism">scientific socialism</a>" as the country's constitutional ideology.<sup id="cite_ref-shillington301_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shillington301-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1965, Congo established relations with the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">People's Republic of China</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-shillington301_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shillington301-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Massamba-Débat was unable to reconcile various institutional and ideological factions<sup id="cite_ref-shillington301_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shillington301-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> and his regime ended abruptly with a bloodless <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> in August 1968.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marien_Ngouabi.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3a/Marien_Ngouabi.jpg/220px-Marien_Ngouabi.jpg" width="220" height="207" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/3/3a/Marien_Ngouabi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/3/3a/Marien_Ngouabi.jpg 2x" data-file-width="301" data-file-height="283" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marien_Ngouabi.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Marien_Ngouabi" title="Marien Ngouabi">Marien Ngouabi</a> changed the country's name to the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="People's Republic of the Congo">People's Republic of the Congo</a>, declaring it to be <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>'s first Marxist–Leninist state and was assassinated in 1977.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Marien_Ngouabi" title="Marien Ngouabi">Marien Ngouabi</a>, who had participated in the coup, assumed the presidency on December 31, 1968. One year later, President Ngouabi proclaimed Congo Africa's first "people's republic", the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="People's Republic of the Congo">People's Republic of the Congo</a>, and announced the decision of the National Revolutionary Movement to change its name to the <a href="/wiki/Congolese_Party_of_Labour" title="Congolese Party of Labour">Congolese Labour Party</a> (PCT). On March 16, 1977, President Ngouabi was assassinated. An 11-member Military Committee of the Party (CMP) was named to head an interim government with <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Yhombi-Opango" title="Joachim Yhombi-Opango">Joachim Yhombi-Opango</a> to serve as President of the Republic. Two years later, Yhombi-Opango was forced from power and <a href="/wiki/Denis_Sassou_Nguesso" title="Denis Sassou Nguesso">Denis Sassou Nguesso</a> become the new president.<sup id="cite_ref-dos2009_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dos2009-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Sassou Nguesso aligned the country with the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Bloc" title="Eastern Bloc">Eastern Bloc</a> and signed a twenty-year friendship pact with the Soviet Union. Over the years, Sassou had to rely more on <a href="/wiki/Political_repression" title="Political repression">political repression</a> and less on <a href="/wiki/Patronage" title="Patronage">patronage</a> to maintain his dictatorship.<sup id="cite_ref-shillington302_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shillington302-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pascal_Lissouba" title="Pascal Lissouba">Pascal Lissouba</a>, who became Congo's first elected president (1992–1997) during the period of multi-party democracy, attempted to implement economic reforms with IMF backing to liberalise the economy. In June 1996 the IMF approved a three-year <a href="/wiki/Special_drawing_rights" title="Special drawing rights">SDR</a>69.5m (US$100m) enhanced structural adjustment facility (ESAF) and was on the verge of announcing a renewed annual agreement when civil war broke out in Congo in mid-1997.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Congo's democratic progress was derailed in 1997 when Lissouba and Sassou started to fight for power in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo_Civil_War" title="Republic of the Congo Civil War">civil war</a>. As presidential elections scheduled for July 1997 approached, tensions between the Lissouba and Sassou camps mounted. On June 5, President Lissouba's government forces surrounded Sassou's compound in Brazzaville and Sassou ordered members of his private militia (known as "Cobras") to resist. Thus began a four-month conflict that destroyed or damaged much of Brazzaville and caused tens of thousands of civilian deaths. In early October, the Angolan socialist régime began an invasion of Congo to install Sassou in power. In mid-October, the Lissouba government fell. Soon thereafter, Sassou declared himself president.<sup id="cite_ref-dos2009_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dos2009-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the controversial <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo_presidential_election,_2002" title="Republic of the Congo presidential election, 2002">elections in 2002</a>, Sassou won with almost 90% of the vote cast. His two main rivals, Lissouba and Bernard Kolelas, were prevented from competing and the only remaining credible rival, <a href="/wiki/Andre_Milongo" title="Andre Milongo" class="mw-redirect">Andre Milongo</a>, advised his supporters to boycott the elections and then withdrew from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-freedomhouse_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-freedomhouse-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> A new <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a>, agreed upon by <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo_constitutional_referendum,_2002" title="Republic of the Congo constitutional referendum, 2002">referendum in January 2002</a>, granted the president new powers, extended his term to seven years, and introduced a new bicameral assembly. International observers took issue with the organization of the presidential election and the constitutional referendum, both of which were reminiscent in their organization of Congo's era of the single-party state.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Following the presidential elections, fighting restarted in the <a href="/wiki/Pool_Department" title="Pool Department">Pool region</a> between government forces and rebels led by <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Ntumi" title="Pastor Ntumi" class="mw-redirect">Pastor Ntumi</a>; a peace treaty to end the conflict was signed in April 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Sassou also won the following <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo_presidential_election,_2009" title="Republic of the Congo presidential election, 2009">presidential election in July 2009</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the Congolese Observatory of Human Rights, a non-governmental organization, the election was marked by "very low" turnout and "fraud and irregularities".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Government and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Denis_Sassou-Nguesso.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Denis_Sassou-Nguesso.jpg/220px-Denis_Sassou-Nguesso.jpg" width="220" height="324" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/48/Denis_Sassou-Nguesso.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/48/Denis_Sassou-Nguesso.jpg 2x" data-file-width="316" data-file-height="466" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Denis_Sassou-Nguesso.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Denis_Sassou_Nguesso" title="Denis Sassou Nguesso">Denis Sassou Nguesso</a> served as President from 1979 to 1992 and has remained in power ever since his rebel forces ousted President <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Lissouba" title="Pascal Lissouba">Pascal Lissouba</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo_Civil_War" title="Republic of the Congo Civil War">1997 Civil War</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Politics of the Republic of the Congo">Politics of the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<div class="hatnote boilerplate seealso">See also: <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Foreign relations of the Republic of the Congo">Foreign relations of the Republic of the Congo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Military_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Military of the Republic of the Congo" class="mw-redirect">Military of the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<p>Congo-Brazzaville has had a multi-party political system since the early 1990s, although the system is heavily dominated by President <a href="/wiki/Denis_Sassou_Nguesso" title="Denis Sassou Nguesso">Denis Sassou Nguesso</a>; he has lacked serious competition in the presidential elections held under his rule. Sassou Nguesso is backed by his own <a href="/wiki/Congolese_Labour_Party" title="Congolese Labour Party" class="mw-redirect">Congolese Labour Party</a> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>Parti Congolais du Travail</i></span>) as well as a range of smaller parties.</p>
<p>Internationally, Sassou's regime has been hit by corruption revelations despite attempts to censor them. One French investigation found over 110 bank accounts and dozens of lavish properties in France; Sassou denounced embezzlement investigations as "racist" and "colonial".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Media_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Media of the Republic of the Congo">Media of the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<p>In 2008, the main media were owned by the government, but many more privately run forms of media were being created. There is one government-owned television station and around 10 small private television channels.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_rights">Human rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Human rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Human_rights_in_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Human rights in the Republic of the Congo">Human rights in the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Pygmies" title="Pygmies" class="mw-redirect">Pygmies</a> belong from birth to Bantus in a relationship many refer to as slavery.<sup id="cite_ref-globalpost_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globalpost-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-internationalreportingproject_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationalreportingproject-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> The Congolese Human Rights Observatory claims that the Pygmies are treated as property the same way "pets" are.<sup id="cite_ref-globalpost_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globalpost-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> On December 30, 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Parliament of the Republic of the Congo">Congolese parliament</a> adopted a law for the promotion and protection of the rights of indigenous peoples. This law is the first of its kind in Africa, and its adoption is a historic development for indigenous peoples on the continent.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Geography of the Republic of the Congo">Geography of the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Congo_republic_sm04.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Congo_republic_sm04.png/220px-Congo_republic_sm04.png" width="220" height="237" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4e/Congo_republic_sm04.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4e/Congo_republic_sm04.png 2x" data-file-width="326" data-file-height="351" /></a>

<p>Congo is located in the central-western part of <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a>, along the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>, lying between latitudes <a href="/wiki/4th_parallel_north" title="4th parallel north">4°N</a> and <a href="/wiki/5th_parallel_south" title="5th parallel south">5°S</a>, and longitudes <a href="/wiki/11th_meridian_east" title="11th meridian east">11°</a> and <a href="/wiki/19th_meridian_east" title="19th meridian east">19°E</a>. To the south and east of it is the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of Congo" class="mw-redirect">Democratic Republic of Congo</a>. It is also bounded by <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> to the north, and <a href="/wiki/Cabinda_(province)" title="Cabinda (province)" class="mw-redirect">Cabinda</a> (<a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>) to the southwest. It has a short coast on the Atlantic Ocean.</p>
<p>The capital, <a href="/wiki/Brazzaville" title="Brazzaville">Brazzaville</a>, is located on the Congo River, in the south of the country, immediately across from <a href="/wiki/Kinshasa" title="Kinshasa">Kinshasa</a>, the capital of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.</p>
<p>The southwest of the country is a coastal plain for which the primary drainage is the <a href="/wiki/Kouilou-Niari_River" title="Kouilou-Niari River">Kouilou-Niari River</a>; the interior of the country consists of a central plateau between two basins to the south and north. Forests are under increasing exploitation pressure.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Since the country is located on the Equator, the climate is consistent year-round, with the average day temperature being a humid 24&#160;°C (75&#160;°F) and nights generally between 16&#160;°C (61&#160;°F) and 21&#160;°C (70&#160;°F). The average yearly rainfall ranges from 1,100 millimetres (43&#160;in) in south in the <a href="/wiki/Kouilou-Niari_River" title="Kouilou-Niari River">Niari</a> valley to over 2,000 millimetres (79&#160;in) in central parts of the country. The dry season is from June to August while in the majority of the country the wet season has two rainfall maxima: one in March–May and another in September–November.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006–07, researchers from the <a href="/wiki/Wildlife_Conservation_Society" title="Wildlife Conservation Society">Wildlife Conservation Society</a> studied gorillas in heavily forested regions centered on the Ouesso district of the <a href="/wiki/Sangha_Region" title="Sangha Region" class="mw-redirect">Sangha Region</a>. They suggest a population on the order of 125,000 <a href="/wiki/Western_Lowland_Gorilla" title="Western Lowland Gorilla" class="mw-redirect">Western Lowland Gorillas</a>, whose isolation from humans has been largely preserved by inhospitable swamps.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Economy of the Republic of the Congo">Economy of the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<div class="hatnote boilerplate seealso">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_companies_based_in_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="List of companies based in the Republic of the Congo">List of companies based in the Republic of the Congo</a></div>

<p>The economy is a mixture of village agriculture and handicrafts, an industrial sector based largely on <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-eia_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eia-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> support services, and a government characterized by budget problems and overstaffing. Petroleum extraction has supplanted <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> as the mainstay of the economy. In 2008, oil sector accounted for 65% of the GDP, 85% of government revenue, and 92% of exports.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> The country also has large untapped <a href="/wiki/Mining_in_the_Republic_of_Congo" title="Mining in the Republic of Congo">mineral</a> wealth.</p>
<p>In the early 1980s, rapidly rising oil revenues enabled the government to finance large-scale development projects with GDP growth averaging 5% annually, one of the highest rates in Africa. The government has mortgaged a substantial portion of its petroleum earnings, contributing to a shortage of revenues. The January 12, 1994 devaluation of <a href="/wiki/CFA_franc" title="CFA franc">Franc Zone</a> currencies by 50% resulted in inflation of 46% in 1994, but inflation has subsided since.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

Young women learning to sew, Brazzaville</div>

<p>Economic reform efforts continued with the support of international organizations, notably the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a>. The reform program came to a halt in June 1997 when civil war erupted. When Sassou Nguesso returned to power at the end of the war in October 1997, he publicly expressed interest in moving forward on economic reforms and privatization and in renewing cooperation with international financial institutions. However, economic progress was badly hurt by slumping oil prices and the resumption of armed conflict in December 1998, which worsened the republic's budget deficit.</p>
<p>The current administration presides over an uneasy internal peace and faces difficult economic problems of stimulating recovery and reducing poverty, despite record-high oil prices since 2003. Natural gas and diamonds are also recent major Congolese exports, although Congo was excluded from the <a href="/wiki/Kimberley_Process" title="Kimberley Process" class="mw-redirect">Kimberley Process</a> in 2004 amid allegations that most of its diamond exports were in fact being smuggled out of the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of Congo" class="mw-redirect">Democratic Republic of Congo</a>; it was re-admitted to the group in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Republic of the Congo also has large untapped base metal, gold, iron and phosphate deposits.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> The country is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Organization_for_the_Harmonization_of_Business_Law_in_Africa" title="Organization for the Harmonization of Business Law in Africa" class="mw-redirect">Organization for the Harmonization of Business Law in Africa</a> (OHADA).<sup id="cite_ref-ohada.com_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ohada.com-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> The Congolese government signed an agreement in 2009 to lease 200,000 hectares of land to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South African</a> farmers to reduce its dependence on imports.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg/300px-Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg" width="300" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg/450px-Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg/600px-Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1194" data-file-height="596" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aeroport_Maya-Maya.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Maya-Maya_Airport" title="Maya-Maya Airport">Maya-Maya Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brazzaville" title="Brazzaville">Brazzaville</a>.</div>

<p>Transport in the Republic of the Congo includes land, air and water transportation. The country's <a href="/wiki/Congo%E2%80%93Ocean_Railway" title="Congo–Ocean Railway">rail system</a> was built by forced laborers during the 1930s and largely remains in operation. There are also over 1000&#160;km of paved roads and two major international airports (<a href="/wiki/Maya-Maya_Airport" title="Maya-Maya Airport">Maya-Maya Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pointe_Noire_Airport" title="Pointe Noire Airport">Pointe Noire Airport</a>) which have flights to Paris and many African cities. The country also has a large port on the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> at <a href="/wiki/Pointe-Noire" title="Pointe-Noire">Pointe-Noire</a> and others along the <a href="/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a> at <a href="/wiki/Brazzaville" title="Brazzaville">Brazzaville</a> and <a href="/wiki/Impfondo" title="Impfondo">Impfondo</a>.</p>

<p>The Republic of the Congo's sparse population is concentrated in the southwestern portion of the country, leaving the vast areas of tropical <a href="/wiki/Jungle" title="Jungle">jungle</a> in the north virtually uninhabited. Thus, Congo is one of the most urbanized countries in Africa, with 70% of its total population living in a few urban areas, namely in <a href="/wiki/Brazzaville" title="Brazzaville">Brazzaville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pointe-Noire" title="Pointe-Noire">Pointe-Noire</a> or one of the small cities or villages lining the 534-kilometre (332&#160;mi) railway which connects the two cities. In rural areas, industrial and commercial activity has declined rapidly in recent years, leaving rural economies dependent on the government for support and subsistence.<sup id="cite_ref-StateDept_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StateDept-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Ethnically and linguistically the population of the Republic of the Congo is diverse—<a href="/wiki/Ethnologue" title="Ethnologue">Ethnologue</a> recognises 62 spoken languages in the country<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup>—but can be grouped into three categories. The <a href="/wiki/Kongo_people" title="Kongo people">Kongo</a> are the largest ethnic group and form roughly half of the population. The most significant subgroups of the Kongo are <a href="/wiki/Lari_(ethnic_group)" title="Lari (ethnic group)" class="mw-redirect">Laari</a> in Brazzaville and Pool regions and Vili around Pointe-Noire and along the Atlantic coast. The second largest group are the <a href="/wiki/Bateke" title="Bateke" class="mw-redirect">Teke</a> who live to the north of Brazzaville with 17% of the population. <a href="/wiki/Mbochi" title="Mbochi" class="mw-redirect">Boulangui</a> (M’Boshi) live in northwest and in Brazzaville and form 12% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pygmies" title="Pygmies" class="mw-redirect">Pygmies</a> make up 2% of Congo's population.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Before the 1997 war, about 9,000 <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a> and other non-Africans lived in Congo, most of whom were <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a>; only a fraction of this number remains.<sup id="cite_ref-StateDept_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StateDept-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> Around 300 <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> expatriates reside in the Congo.<sup id="cite_ref-StateDept_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StateDept-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The people of Republic of the Congo are largely a mix of <a href="/wiki/Catholics" title="Catholics" class="mw-redirect">Catholics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Protestants" title="Protestants" class="mw-redirect">Protestants</a>, who collectively account for 50.0%. The majority of Christians in the country are Catholic, while the remaining comprises various other Christian denominations. Followers of <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> make up less than 2% of the population, and this is primarily due to an influx of foreign workers into the urban centers.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>According to a 2011–12 survey, total fertility rate was 5.1 with 4.5 in urban and 6.5 in rural.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Health_in_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Health in the Republic of the Congo">Health in the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<p>Public expenditure health was at 8.9% of the GDP in 2004, whereas private expenditure was at 1.3%.<sup id="cite_ref-hdrstats.undp.org_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdrstats.undp.org-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> As of 2012, the <a href="/wiki/HIV/AIDS" title="HIV/AIDS">HIV/AIDS</a> prevalence was at 2.8% among 15- to 49-year-olds.<sup id="cite_ref-cia.gov_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia.gov-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> Health expenditure was at US$ 30 per capita in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-hdrstats.undp.org_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdrstats.undp.org-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> A large proportion of the population is undernourished,<sup id="cite_ref-hdrstats.undp.org_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdrstats.undp.org-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> with malnutrition being a problem in Congo-Brazzaville.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> There were 20 physicians per 100,000 persons in the early 2000s (decade).<sup id="cite_ref-hdrstats.undp.org_47-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdrstats.undp.org-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Maternal_mortality" title="Maternal mortality" class="mw-redirect">maternal mortality</a> rate was 560 deaths/100,000 live births, and the <a href="/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">infant mortality</a> rate was 59.34 deaths/1,000 live births.<sup id="cite_ref-cia.gov_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia.gov-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Female_genital_mutilation" title="Female genital mutilation">Female genital mutilation</a> (FGM) is rare in the country, being confined to limited geographic areas of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Culture of the Republic of the Congo">Culture of the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Education in the Republic of the Congo">Education in the Republic of the Congo</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg/220px-SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg/330px-SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg/440px-SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="784" data-file-height="588" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SAINTE_RITA_CONG-BR2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
School children in the classroom, Republic of the Congo</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Public expenditure of the GDP was less in 2002–05 than in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-hdrstats.undp.org_47-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdrstats.undp.org-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> Public education is theoretically free and mandatory for under-16-year-olds,<sup id="cite_ref-unhcr.org_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unhcr.org-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> but in practice, expenses exist.<sup id="cite_ref-unhcr.org_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unhcr.org-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> Net primary enrollment rate was 44% in 2005, much less than the 79% in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-hdrstats.undp.org_47-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdrstats.undp.org-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> The country has universities. Education between ages six and sixteen is compulsory. Pupils who complete six years of primary school and seven years of secondary school obtain a baccalaureate. At the university, students can obtain a bachelor's degree in three years and a master's after four. Marien Ngouabi University—which offers courses in medicine, law and several other fields—is the country's only public university. Instruction at all levels is in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, and the educational system as a whole models the French system. The educational infrastructure has been seriously degraded as a result of political and economic crises. There are no seats in most classrooms, forcing children to sit on the floor. Enterprising individuals have set up private schools, but they often lack the technical knowledge and familiarity with the national curriculum to teach effectively. Families frequently enroll their children in private schools only to find they cannot make the payments.</p>
